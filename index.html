Plant Propagation Methods

Unit Q1: Name five (5) methods of propagating plants?
Model Answer: 1. Seed propagation, 2. Vegetative cuttings (softwood, semi-hardwood, hardwood), 3. Division, 4. Layering, 5. Grafting/budding. Also: tissue culture, root cuttings, leaf cuttings, spore.

Seed Propagation

For Tasmanian natives: Eucalyptus, Acacia, Banksia, Leptospermum. Pre-treatments: scarification (hot water for Acacia), cold stratification (Pomaderris), smoke water (many heath species).

Cutting Propagation

Softwood (spring), semi-hardwood (summer), hardwood (winter). Tasmanian examples: Leptospermum, Melaleuca, Correa, Prostanthera – use IBA rooting hormone 2000-3000ppm.

Division & Layering

Divide clumping species: Poa, Lomandra, Gahnia, Dianella. Layering works for many shrubs (e.g., Rhododendron, Prostanthera).

Q6: Cuttings are also classified based on the plant parts that they come from. List four (4) plant parts that can be used for cuttings.
Answer: 1. Stem cuttings, 2. Leaf cuttings, 3. Root cuttings, 4. Leaf-bud cuttings (also leaf vein, etc.).
Q8: Name two (2) types of containers used for sticking cuttings.
Answer: Tubes, cell trays, plug trays, kwik trays.

Planning, Parent Plants & Ethics

Q2: a) What is a parent plant? b) What qualities are required of parent plants?
Model Answer: a) The plant from which you collect cuttings or seeds. b) Should be young, vigorously growing, healthy, pest/disease free, true to type, and not governed by Plant Breeder’s Rights.
Q3: When is the best time of day to collect vegetative material and why?
Answer: Early morning, when the plant is turgid (fully hydrated).
Q4: Describe three (3) storage methods to preserve vegetative material once separated from parent plant.
Answer: 1. Polythene bags in cool store; 2. Buckets of water or wet sphagnum/coir; 3. Refrigerated esky; 4. Wrapped in wet newspaper and bagged.
Q14: What are five (5) ways to maintain hygiene in a propagation area?
Answer: 1. Sterilise workbench/tools/pots; 2. Clean floors; 3. Do not bring soil into propagation area, use footbaths; 4. Wear gloves; 5. Metho spray; 6. Isolate new plants/quarantine.
Q16: How does the application of nutrients differ with propagation stages?
Answer: Seeds/cuttings without roots: no nutrition required. Once germinated or rooted: introduce low-phosphorus liquid fertiliser (¼ strength) for native species.

Collection, Hormones & Equipment

Q7: Name two (2) reasons why cuttings are dipped into a hormone treatment.
Answer: 1. To hasten root initiation; 2. Increase percentage of cuttings that form roots; 3. Increase number/quality of roots; 4. Increase uniformity of rooting.
Q9: Name three (3) reasons why cuttings are placed on a controlled heat bed and kept misted.
Answer: 1. Keep tops cool/moist; 2. Reduce drying/wilting while roots initiate; 3. Encourage active cell division and initiate roots.
Q10: What environmental factors are required for seeds to germinate?
Answer: Moisture, temperature, oxygen, and sometimes light.
Q11: Briefly explain cold and warm stratification with examples.
Answer: Cold stratification: moist cold treatment (-4 to 7°C) – e.g., Pomaderris apetala. Warm stratification: warm moist soil (7-20°C) – e.g., tropical palms.
Q12: Name two types of scarification and examples.
Answer: 1. Boiling water – Acacia; 2. Abrasion/filing – Hardenbergia; also burning (hard seed).
Q17: Name four (4) things for maintaining and storing propagation tools.
Answer: Clean and sterilise before/after use; sharpen blades; lubricate tools; store in dry dust-free place.

Aftercare, Hygiene & Monitoring

Q13: What are five (5) common problems during establishment in greenhouse?
Answer: Seeds won’t germinate, cuttings wilt/die, cuttings won’t root, over/under watering, pests/diseases (damping off, root rots, Botrytis, aphids, fungus gnats).
Q15: How does nutrient application differ between stages? (see Q16 also) – already covered.
Q18: Disposal methods for nursery waste.
Answer: Broken pots – landfill/recycling; diseased material – landfill/solarisation; used media – compost; green waste – compost; weeds with seeds – landfill/weed tea/solarisation.
Q19: Name three (3) records kept in a propagation nursery.
Answer: Propagation records, potting records, daily temperature records, spray records, fertiliser records.
Q20: List four (4) daily checks in a propagation greenhouse.
Answer: Bed temps, air temp (max/min), humidity levels, misting operation, media moisture, light levels, pest/disease observation.

Keep tops cool/moist; reduce wilting; encourage root initiation.

Moisture, temperature, oxygen, sometimes light.

Cold: moist cold (Pomaderris); Warm: warm moist soil (tropical palms).

Boiling water (Acacia); abrasion/filing (Hardenbergia).

Glossary & Key Resources

Orthodox seedCan be dried and frozen (e.g., Eucalyptus, Acacia). Recalcitrant seedCannot be dried; sow fresh (e.g., Nothofagus). StratificationCold/moist treatment to break dormancy. ScarificationMechanical/hot water treatment to allow water uptake. ProvenanceGeographic origin of propagation material – critical for restoration.
