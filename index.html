Five Core Propagation Methods

1. Seed Propagation

Sexual propagation: genetic diversity. Pre-treatments: scarification (Acacia, Kennedia), cold stratification (Pomaderris, Eucalyptus coccifera), smoke water (Banksia, Stylidium). Sow in sterile media, depth = 2-3× seed diameter.

Tasmanian examples: Eucalyptus globulus, Nothofagus cunninghamii, Banksia marginata

2. Vegetative Cuttings

Softwood (spring): Leptospermum, Melaleuca – IBA 2000ppm.
Semi-hardwood (summer): Correa, Prostanthera – IBA 3000ppm.
Hardwood (winter): deciduous species. High humidity + bottom heat 22-25°C.

3. Division & Layering

Division: Poa labillardierei, Lomandra longifolia, Gahnia grandis, Dianella tasmanica. Divide in spring, ensure each piece has roots + shoots.
Layering: Peg low branch, cover with soil; sever when rooted.

4. Grafting & Budding

Used for cultivars and difficult-to-root species (e.g., some eucalypts, ornamental natives). Whip & tongue, cleft graft. Match cambium, seal with grafting tape.

5. Spore & Tissue Culture

Ferns: collect spores, sow on sterile medium. Tissue culture for mass propagation of threatened species (e.g., some orchids).

Assessment Knowledge: Five Methods

The unit requires identification of five propagation methods: seed, cutting, division, layering, grafting. Each has specific applications for Tasmanian native plants.

Model Answer (Q1): 1. Seed propagation, 2. Vegetative cuttings (softwood, semi-hardwood, hardwood), 3. Division, 4. Layering, 5. Grafting/budding. Also: tissue culture, root cuttings, spore.

Work Planning & Ethical Sourcing

Parent Plant Selection

Qualities: Young, vigorously growing, healthy, pest/disease free, true to type, not under Plant Breeder’s Rights. Avoid stressed or flowering plants.

Q2: a) Parent plant = source of cuttings/seed. b) Qualities: young, vigorous, healthy, true-to-type, free of pests/disease, not PBR-protected.

Best Collection Time

Early morning – plants are turgid (fully hydrated). Avoid midday heat.

Q3: Early morning, when plant is turgid (fully hydrated).

Storage Methods for Vegetative Material

1. Polythene bags in cool store (2-5°C). 2. Buckets of water or wet sphagnum/coir. 3. Refrigerated esky. 4. Wrapped in wet newspaper and sealed.

Q4: In polythene bags in cool store; buckets of water/wet sphagnum; refrigerated esky; wet newspaper + bagged.

Hygiene Protocols (5 ways)

1. Sterilise benches/tools (70% ethanol). 2. Clean floors daily. 3. Footbaths at entrance. 4. Wear disposable gloves. 5. Isolate new plants/quarantine. Also: use sterile media, no soil from outside.

Q14: Sterilise work bench/tools; clean floors; footbaths; wear gloves; isolate new plants.

Ethical Collection Guidelines (Tasmania)

Under Nature Conservation Act 2002 and Threatened Species Protection Act 1995, collection from public land or for threatened species requires a permit. Follow the 20% rule: collect no more than 20% of seed/cuttings from any population. Respect Aboriginal heritage.

Propagation Techniques & Equipment

Rooting Hormone (IBA)

Reasons: hasten root initiation, increase rooting percentage, improve root quality, uniformity.

Q7: To hasten root initiation; increase percentage of cuttings that form roots; increase number/quality of roots; uniformity of rooting.

Heat Bed & Misting

1. Keep tops cool/moist. 2. Reduce wilting while roots initiate. 3. Encourage cell division and rooting.

Q9: Keep tops cool/moist; reduce drying/wilting; encourage active cell division and root initiation.

Containers for Cuttings

Tubes, cell trays, plug trays, kwik trays, deep pots for long roots.

Q8: Tubes, cell trays, plug trays, kwik trays.

Seed Germination Factors

Moisture, temperature, oxygen, sometimes light.

Q10: Moisture, temperature, oxygen, and sometimes light.

Tool Maintenance

Clean & sterilise before/after use; sharpen blades; lubricate moving parts; store in dry dust-free place.

Q17: Clean and sterilise; sharpen blades; lubricate; store dry.

Aftercare, Nutrition & Monitoring

Nutrient Application Stages

Before roots: No fertiliser (seeds/cuttings). After rooting/germination: Dilute liquid fertiliser (¼ strength) with low phosphorus for natives.

Q15/16: Seeds/cuttings without roots require no nutrition. Once germinated/rooted, introduce dilute low-P fertiliser.

Five Common Problems

1. Seeds fail to germinate. 2. Cuttings wilt/die. 3. Cuttings won’t root. 4. Over/under watering. 5. Pests/diseases (damping off, Botrytis, aphids, fungus gnats).

Q13: Seeds won’t germinate, cuttings wilt/die, cuttings won’t root, over/under watering, pests/diseases (damping off, root rots, Botrytis, aphids, fungus gnats).

Nursery Waste Disposal

Broken pots: landfill/recycling. Diseased material: landfill/solarisation. Used media: compost. Green waste: compost. Weeds with seeds: landfill/weed tea/solarisation.

Q18: Broken pots → landfill/recycling; diseased → landfill/solarisation; used media → compost; green waste → compost; weeds with seeds → landfill/weed tea.

Records Kept in Nursery

Propagation records, potting records, daily temperature records, spray records, fertiliser records.

Q19: Propagation records, potting records, daily temperature records, spray records, fertiliser records.

Daily Greenhouse Checks

1. Bed temperatures. 2. Air temperature (max/min). 3. Humidity levels. 4. Misting operation / media moisture. 5. Light levels. 6. Pest/disease observation.

Q20: Bed temps, air temp, humidity levels, misting operation, media moisture, light levels, pest/disease observation.
