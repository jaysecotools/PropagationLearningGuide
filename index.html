1. Seed Propagation

Tasmanian natives: Eucalyptus globulus, Acacia melanoxylon, Banksia marginata, Nothofagus cunninghamii

Pre-treatments:
• Hot water scarification: Acacia, Kennedia (pour boiling water, soak 12-24h)
• Cold stratification: Pomaderris, Eucalyptus coccifera (2-5°C, 6-10 weeks)
• Smoke water: Banksia, Stylidium, many heath species
• Sowing depth: 2-3× seed diameter; fine seeds surface sow

Media: Sterile seed-raising mix, low phosphorus for natives.

Q1 (5 methods): Seed, cutting, division, layering, grafting. Q10: Moisture, temperature, oxygen, sometimes light.
2. Vegetative Cuttings

Softwood cuttings (spring-early summer): Leptospermum, Melaleuca, Correa – IBA 2000-3000ppm, high humidity.
Semi-hardwood (late summer-autumn): Prostanthera, Westringia – IBA 3000ppm, mist.
Hardwood (winter): deciduous species, some eucalypts from juvenile stock.
Rooting hormone (IBA): hastens rooting, increases percentage, improves root quality and uniformity.

Q5: Softwood (spring), semi-hardwood (late summer), hardwood (winter). Q6: Stem, leaf, root, leaf-bud cuttings. Q7: Hasten root initiation; increase rooting %; improve root quality; uniformity.
3. Division & Layering

Division: Ideal for clumping species: Poa labillardierei, Lomandra longifolia, Gahnia grandis, Dianella tasmanica, Dicksonia antarctica. Divide in early spring, ensure each division has 3-5 healthy shoots and roots.
Layering: Peg low branch into soil, cover, sever when rooted. Works for Rhododendron, Prostanthera, Rhododendron.
Propagation from suckers: Some eucalypts produce lignotuber shoots.

4. Grafting & Budding

Used for cultivars and difficult-to-root species. Whip & tongue for equal diameter scion/rootstock; cleft graft for larger rootstock. T-budding for roses, some natives.
Key principles: Match cambium layers, seal with grafting tape/wax, keep humid until callus forms.
Tasmanian context: Grafting of Eucalyptus cultivars, Telopea truncata for improved flowering.

⏰ Best Collection Time & Storage

Early morning – plants are turgid (fully hydrated). Avoid midday heat and wilting.
Storage methods:
• Polythene bags in cool store (2-5°C)
• Buckets of water or wet sphagnum moss
• Refrigerated esky with ice bricks
• Wrapped in wet newspaper and sealed in plastic

Q3: Early morning, when plant is turgid. Q4: Polythene bags cool store; buckets of water/wet sphagnum; refrigerated esky; wet newspaper + bagged.
🌱 Seed Dormancy Treatments

Scarification (physical dormancy): Hot water (Acacia, Kennedia), abrasion (Hardenbergia), nicking (Pultenaea).
Stratification (physiological dormancy): Cold stratification (Pomaderris, Eucalyptus coccifera) 4-10 weeks at 2-5°C.
Smoke treatment: Soak in smoke water or use smoke disks for fire-responsive species (Banksia, Stylidium, Actinotus).

Q11: Cold stratification: moist cold (Pomaderris); Warm stratification: warm moist soil (tropical palms). Q12: Boiling water (Acacia); abrasion (Hardenbergia).

Work Planning, Parent Plants & Ethical Sourcing

🌿 Parent Plant Selection (Q2)

Definition: The plant from which propagation material (cuttings, seed, divisions) is collected.
Required qualities:
• Young, vigorous growth habit
• Healthy, pest and disease free
• True to type (genetically true to species/cultivar)
• Not governed by Plant Breeder’s Rights (PBR)
• Well-watered the day before collection
• Avoid plants in flower or fruiting (for cuttings)

Q2a: Parent plant = source plant. Q2b: Young, vigorous, healthy, pest-free, true-to-type, not PBR.
🧼 Hygiene Protocols (5 ways - Q14)

1. Sterilise equipment: 70% ethanol or 1% bleach solution on benches, tools, pots.
2. Clean floors: Daily sweeping and disinfecting.
3. Footbaths: At propagation area entrance to prevent pathogen spread.
4. Personal hygiene: Wear disposable gloves, wash hands, clean overalls.
5. Quarantine: Isolate new plant material for 2 weeks, separate propagation zones.
Additional: Use sterile propagation media, avoid bringing soil into greenhouse.

Q14: Sterilise benches/tools; clean floors; footbaths; wear gloves; isolate new plants/quarantine.

Ethical Collection Guidelines & Permits (Tasmania)

Legislation: Under Nature Conservation Act 2002 and Threatened Species Protection Act 1995, collection of native plant material from public land or for threatened species requires a scientific permit from NRE Tas.
The 20% Rule: Collect no more than 20% of seed/cuttings from any population to ensure natural regeneration and maintain genetic diversity.
Permit contacts: FloraPermit.Enquiries@nre.tas.gov.au
Aboriginal heritage: Due diligence required before ground-disturbing works.

📦 Storage of Collected Material

Immediate post-collection:
• Place in sealed polythene bags with damp paper towel
• Keep in cool, shaded area
• Transport in insulated esky with ice bricks
• Process within 4-6 hours for optimal viability
Refrigeration: Store at 2-5°C for up to 48 hours if processing delayed.

Propagation Techniques, Equipment & Maintenance

🧪 Rooting Hormone (IBA) - Q7

Why use rooting hormone?
1. Hastens root initiation
2. Increases percentage of cuttings that form roots
3. Increases number and quality of roots per cutting
4. Improves uniformity of rooting
Application: Dip base of cutting (1-2cm) into IBA powder/gel immediately before inserting into media. Use fresh hormone, avoid contamination.

Q7: Hasten root initiation; increase % cuttings that root; increase number/quality of roots; uniformity.
🔥 Heat Bed & Misting Systems - Q9

Reasons for bottom heat:
1. Encourages active cell division at cutting base
2. Promotes faster root initiation (optimal 22-25°C)
Reasons for misting:
1. Keeps tops cool and reduces transpiration
2. Prevents wilting while roots develop
3. Maintains high humidity (85-95%) around foliage
Timing: Intermittent mist (5-15 sec every 10-30 min) depending on conditions.

Q9: Keep tops cool/moist; reduce drying/wilting; encourage active cell division and root initiation.

🌱 Seed Germination Factors - Q10

Essential factors:
• Moisture: Consistent but not waterlogged
• Temperature: Optimal varies by species (15-25°C for most natives)
• Oxygen: Well-aerated media prevents damping off
• Light: Some species require light for germination (surface sow), others darkness (cover with vermiculite)

Q10: Moisture, temperature, oxygen, sometimes light.
🧫 Containers for Cuttings - Q8

Common container types:
• Tubes: 50mm, 75mm, 100mm depths for deep-rooted species
• Cell trays: 40-cell, 50-cell, 84-cell for high-density propagation
• Plug trays: For automated filling and transplanting
• Kwik trays: Rigid trays with individual cells
• Root trainers: Air-pruning design for eucalypts and acacias

Q8: Tubes, cell trays, plug trays, kwik trays.

Aftercare, Nutrition & Environmental Monitoring

💧 Nutrient Application Stages - Q15/Q16

Before roots develop (seeds not germinated / cuttings not rooted):
• No fertiliser required
• Seeds contain endosperm for initial growth
• Cuttings have no root system to uptake nutrients
After germination / root development:
• Introduce dilute liquid fertiliser (¼ strength)
• Use low-phosphorus formula for native plants (Proteaceae sensitive)
• Apply weekly or fortnightly during active growth

Q15/16: Seeds/cuttings without roots require no nutrition. Once germinated/rooted, introduce dilute low-P fertiliser.
🐛 Common Problems in Greenhouse - Q13

Five common problems:
1. Seeds won't germinate – check dormancy requirements, temperature, moisture
2. Cuttings wilt or die – inadequate humidity, poor parent material, pathogens
3. Cuttings won't root – insufficient hormone, wrong temperature, poor timing
4. Over or under watering – monitor media moisture, adjust misting
5. Pests and diseases – damping-off (fungal), Botrytis, root rots, aphids, fungus gnats

Q13: Seeds won’t germinate; cuttings wilt/die; cuttings won’t root; over/under watering; pests/diseases (damping off, Botrytis, aphids, fungus gnats).

🗑️ Nursery Waste Disposal - Q18

Broken pots, trays, tubes: Landfill or recycling (where facilities exist)
Diseased plant material: Landfill, or solarisation in sealed black plastic bags (6 weeks in summer)
Used growing media: Compost or recycle to garden (if disease-free)
General green waste: Compost (hot composting to kill pathogens)
Weeds with seeds, bulbs, rhizomes: Landfill, or weed tea (ferment in water), or solarisation

Q18: Broken pots → landfill/recycling; diseased → landfill/solarisation; used media → compost; green waste → compost; weeds with seeds → landfill/weed tea.
📋 Nursery Records - Q19

Essential records for ASQA compliance:
1. Propagation records: Species, date, method, source, quantity, batch number
2. Potting records: Media type, container size, fertiliser applied, date
3. Daily temperature records: Max/min temperatures, heating system operation
4. Spray records: Pesticide/fungicide applications, rates, dates
5. Fertiliser records: Type, concentration, application dates
6. Environmental monitoring: Humidity, light, mist settings

Q19: Propagation records, potting records, daily temperature records, spray records, fertiliser records.

Glossary & Essential Resources

📖 Botanical Glossary

Orthodox seedCan be dried to 5-8% moisture and stored frozen (e.g., Eucalyptus, Acacia)
Recalcitrant seedCannot be dried; must be sown fresh (e.g., Nothofagus, many rainforest species)
StratificationCold/moist treatment to break physiological dormancy (2-5°C for weeks)
ScarificationMechanical or thermal treatment to break physical seed coat dormancy
ProvenanceGeographic origin of propagation material - critical for restoration success
IBAIndole-3-butyric acid - synthetic rooting hormone
LignotuberWoody swelling at base containing dormant buds (eucalypts)
Damping-offFungal disease killing seedlings at soil line
Hardening offGradual acclimatisation to outdoor conditions
PhytophthoraRoot rot pathogen, major threat to Tasmanian natives
