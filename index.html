Five Core Propagation Methods

1. Seed Propagation

Tasmanian natives: Eucalyptus, Acacia, Banksia, Nothofagus
Key pre-treatments:
• Hot water scarification – Acacia, Kennedia
• Cold stratification – Pomaderris, Eucalyptus coccifera
• Smoke water – Banksia, Stylidium, Richea
Sowing depth: 2-3× seed diameter; fine seeds surface sow.

2. Vegetative Cuttings

Softwood (spring): Leptospermum, Melaleuca – IBA 2000-3000ppm
Semi-hardwood (late summer): Correa, Prostanthera – IBA 3000ppm
Hardwood (winter): deciduous species, some eucalypts
Rooting hormone (IBA): speeds rooting, increases success rate, improves root quality.

3. Division & Layering

Division: Poa labillardierei, Lomandra, Gahnia, Dianella – divide clumps in spring, ensure 3-5 shoots with roots.
Layering: Peg low branch into soil, cover, sever when rooted. Works for many shrubs.

4. Grafting & Budding

Used for cultivars and difficult-to-root species. Whip & tongue, cleft graft. Match cambium layers, seal with grafting tape. Telopea truncata cultivars, some eucalypts.

Study Tip: When preparing for assessment, be able to describe each method with a Tasmanian example. Practice explaining when you would use each method and why.

⏰ Collection Timing & Storage

Best time: Early morning – plants are turgid (fully hydrated).
Storage methods:
• Polythene bags in cool store (2-5°C)
• Buckets of water or wet sphagnum
• Refrigerated esky with ice bricks
• Wrapped in wet newspaper and sealed

🌱 Seed Dormancy Treatments

Scarification (physical): Hot water (Acacia), abrasion (Hardenbergia)
Stratification (physiological): Cold stratification 4-10 weeks (Pomaderris, Eucalyptus coccifera)
Smoke treatment: Fire-responsive species (Banksia, Stylidium)

Work Planning & Ethical Sourcing

🌿 Parent Plant Selection

What to look for:
• Young, vigorous growth
• Healthy, pest and disease free
• True to type (genetically correct)
• Not covered by Plant Breeder's Rights (PBR)
• Well-watered day before collection
• Avoid plants in flower/fruit for cuttings

🧼 Hygiene Protocols

5 key practices:
1. Sterilise benches/tools (70% ethanol)
2. Clean floors daily
3. Footbaths at propagation area entrance
4. Wear disposable gloves
5. Quarantine new plants for 2 weeks
• Use sterile propagation media

Ethical Collection Guidelines (Tasmania)

Legislation: Nature Conservation Act 2002, Threatened Species Protection Act 1995 – permits required for public land or threatened species.
The 20% Rule: Collect no more than 20% of seed/cuttings from any population.
Permit contact: FloraPermit.Enquiries@nre.tas.gov.au

Assessment Preparation: Review the key legislation, understand when permits are required, and explain the 20% rule for sustainable collection.

Propagation Techniques & Equipment

🧪 Rooting Hormone (IBA)

Benefits: Hastens root initiation, increases rooting percentage, improves root quality and uniformity.
Application: Dip base of cutting (1-2cm) into IBA powder/gel immediately before inserting into media. Use fresh, avoid contamination.

🔥 Heat Bed & Misting Systems

Bottom heat (22-25°C): Encourages cell division and root initiation.
Misting: Keeps tops cool, reduces wilting, maintains high humidity (85-95%).
Timing: Intermittent mist – 5-15 sec every 10-30 min depending on conditions.

🌱 Seed Germination Factors

Essential factors:
• Moisture – consistent but not waterlogged
• Temperature – optimal 15-25°C for most natives
• Oxygen – well-aerated media
• Light – some species require light (surface sow), others darkness (cover)

🧫 Containers for Cuttings

Common types: Tubes (50-100mm), cell trays (40-84 cell), plug trays, kwik trays, root trainers. Choose based on species root depth and production volume.

Aftercare, Nutrition & Monitoring

💧 Nutrient Application Stages

Before roots develop: No fertiliser – seeds contain endosperm, cuttings have no root system.
After germination/rooting: Introduce dilute liquid fertiliser (¼ strength). Use low-phosphorus formula for native plants (Proteaceae sensitive).

🐛 Common Problems in Greenhouse

Watch for: Seeds not germinating, cuttings wilting/dying, cuttings not rooting, over/under watering, pests/diseases (damping off, Botrytis, aphids, fungus gnats, root rots).
Prevention: Good hygiene, correct environment, sterile media.

Glossary & Key Resources

📖 Key Terms

Orthodox seed: Can be dried and stored frozen (Eucalyptus, Acacia)
Recalcitrant seed: Cannot be dried; sow fresh (Nothofagus)
Stratification: Cold/moist treatment to break dormancy
Scarification: Mechanical/heat treatment to break seed coat
Provenance: Geographic origin of propagation material
IBA: Indole-3-butyric acid (rooting hormone)
Lignotuber: Woody swelling at base (eucalypts)
Damping-off: Fungal disease killing seedlings
Hardening off: Gradual acclimatisation to outdoors
